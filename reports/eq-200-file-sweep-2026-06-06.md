## 2. Method

### Strata invocation (verbatim, unchanged from v6)

```

  --check-mode deductive --check-level minimal \
  --call-policy bodyOrContract --inline-fuel 100 <core_st>
```

### Driver tuning (the three changes that define this re-run)

| # | Change | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `gtimeout 60` → `gtimeout 20` | The v6 60s budget masked the bimodality (no PASS in v6 took >10s; every >10s outcome was a TIMEOUT). 20s is 2× the v6 max-PASS elapsed, so it preserves all genuine PASSes while cutting per-file TIMEOUT cost to a third. |
| 2 | `ulimit -t 30` (CPU-time cap, 30s) | Substituted for the requested `ulimit -v 8000000`. macOS does not enforce `setrlimit(RLIMIT_AS)` (returns EINVAL on Darwin), so the requested form is a no-op + 1-line warning per worker. CPU-time cap is the closest available equivalent on Darwin. Did NOT have to fire on any of the 200 — the wall-clock cap dominates because strata is CPU-bound. |
| 3 | Small-first ordering (Q1 → Q4) | v6 interleaved sizes; the mem-cap re-run sorts the input list small-first to (a) front-load fast PASSes for early signal and (b) reduce concurrent RSS pressure during the 8-way parallel phase. |

The patched driver is at `/tmp/claude-503/verify-one.sh`; the unified diff is in the Phase 1 patch summary (preserved at `/tmp/claude-503/verify-one.sh.pre`).

### Concurrency

- 8-way parallel via `xargs -n 1 -P 8`.
- Per-worker stdout/stderr at `/tmp/claude-503/eq200-verify-stdout/<base>.out` and `/tmp/claude-503/eq200-verify-stderr/<base>.err`.
- Runner stderr at `/tmp/claude-503/eq200-verify-runner.err` — **0 bytes**, no setrlimit warnings, no driver errors.

### Verdict map

- rc=0 + "Finished with N goals passed, 0 failed" → **PASS**
- "Finished with N goals passed, M failed", M≥1 → **PARTIAL** (no PARTIAL category in the new driver — these collapse to OTHER, see §6)
- rc=3 + "Cannot find this fvar in the context! old …" → **ELAB_FAIL**
- rc=124 (gtimeout fired) → **TIMEOUT**
- rc∈{134, 139} or "stack overflow"/"segfault" in stderr → **SO**
- Otherwise → **OTHER**

The mem-cap driver does not classify PASS_? or PARTIAL separately; both fold into OTHER unless rc=0. This is a **deliberate** simplification — Phase 4 of the v6 sweep already characterized the PARTIAL bimodality (rc=2 `❓ unknown` vs rc=3 SMT-crash) and we don't re-characterize here.

### Wall-clock

- Translate phase: completed before re-run; all 200 .core.st cached at `/tmp/claude-503/eq200-cores/`.
- Verify phase (re-run): **4 min 46 sec** for 200 files at 8-way parallel (200 × 20s wall ÷ 8 = 8 min 20s upper bound; observed 4:46 reflects the 32 instant-PASS + 76 sub-15s files finishing well below the cap).

## 8. Surprises (severity-ranked)

### 1. NONE (positive confirmation) — Tightening the wall budget by 3× (60s → 20s) loses zero PASSes

This is the single load-bearing finding of the re-run. **Every PASS in the 200-file population finishes in ≤4s.** There is no PASS that needs the 60s budget; the 60s budget was strictly inflating per-file cost without buying any verdicts. The 20s budget could in principle be tightened further (the mem-cap max non-TIMEOUT elapsed is 15s for an OTHER-class strata-crash, with 4s for the slowest PASS), but additional tightening would buy at most ~1 minute of wall-clock at n=200 and risks creating a stratification artifact at larger n. **20s is the right operating point.**

### 2. NONE — OOM cluster eliminated

v6's 2 rc=137 SIGKILLs are gone. The host's RSS pressure during v6 was concentrated in the 60s tail; clamping wall-clock at 20s is sufficient to prevent the OS from reaching for the OOM-killer. The Darwin-substituted `ulimit -t 30` is a belt; the gtimeout is the suspenders.

### 3. NONE — `balancedNondetIte` SO fix corpus-stable at 5.7× the closeout's n=28

0/200 at the harder configuration (smaller wall, more RSS pressure simulated by 8-way parallel on 8 GB / 32 GB host). The fix lands.

### 4. NONE — Decimal `e-15` SMT2 fix corpus-stable at the same scale

0/200 e-evidence. The fix lands.

### 5. LOW — ELAB_FAIL rate at 28.0% is above v2 §8.4 by 2.3-3.5×

This is the same surprise the v6 sweep documented (28.5% there, 28.0% here — within sampling noise). The single-class explanation holds: **every ELAB_FAIL is the `old <X>`-of-uncontextualized-fvar pattern**, the lean4 #1331 class. Neither sweep produces a new defect mode; the audit's 21% jsm projection was just an extrapolation from a smaller sample.

### 6. LOW — TIMEOUT bimodality eliminated by tighter cap

v6 reported PASS-with-tail-up-to-60s and TIMEOUT-at-60s. Mem-cap reports PASS-with-tail-up-to-15s and TIMEOUT-at-20s. **Bimodality is now visually obvious** in the elapsed histogram: 32 files at 0s, 76 between 1-15s, and 89 piled at 20-22s. The middle-band (5-15s) is real (43 files) but extremely narrow.
